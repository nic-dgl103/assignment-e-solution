Evaluation of a business' website

I analyzed and evaluated the design of a website below with the Web Design Checklist document. I describe the evaluation from three points of the document, which are “Design with a purpose”, “Write to communicate” and “Create visual hierarchy”.

Design with a purpose

Problem: Each web page does not clearly show the users what to do.

Solution: Clarification of the clinic and users' purposes

The clinic's purpose: They want users to contact them by phone, email, or appointment. User's purpose: They are looking for services of physiotherapy near Comox, because they are in physical trouble.

Problem: The layout is not designed for users to understand the service and book an appointment.

Solution: Consideration of content and layout to achieve those purposes.

Contents to include on each page

Home

  • Self-introduction of the clinic (include link to service details)
  • Introduction of the team (include link to member details page)
  • Link to contact page

Services

  • Prices for each service (list)
  • Explanation of each service (list)
  • Explanation of facilities (photos and explanations)

Contact us

  • Business days and hours
  • Address (with Google Maps)
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Appointment booking

Common content of all webpages

Header
  • Logo of the clinic
  • Navigation
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Appointment booking
Footer
  • Links to SNS
  • Copyright

Write to communicate

Problem: Each content is not grouped into blocks, so it's not easy for users to understand what the clinic to want to do

Solution: Group information into blocks to understand the contents easily.

Problem: These webpages don't have appropriate headings. For example, there is no h1 in “Home” webpage.

Solution: Put appropriate headings on each page.

Problem:
There are some difficult words and long sentences in some webpages.

In “Products & Services” page, although there are links to explain the difficult words, but not all words have links.

In “Our team” page, some long sentences make users give up reading.

Solution: Change difficult words into clear descriptive language and make texts to be short and concise.

If they have to use these difficult words, add links to explain them, which contribute users to understand the words.

Create visual hierarchy

Problem: The size, font weight and colour are not set appropriately.

This confuses users which information is important of each page (Ex, “Rate” page)

Solution: Unify size, font weight and colour.

Problem: These pages have few text colors and don't have appropriate space, so there are no visual hierarchy.

Solution: Create 3 levels of importance.

For example,

  • First: Sentence that guides users to the contact page and the service page
  • Second: headings
  • Third: other content
